# MedCMU Special Mode

Use this module for Faculty of Medicine, Chiang Mai University, Maharaj Nakorn Chiang Mai Hospital, academic medicine in Northern Thailand, or nearby public-health contexts.

## Strategic Context

MedCMU combines:

- Medical faculty authority.
- Teaching hospital credibility.
- Public-service mission.
- Northern Thailand community trust.
- Research and specialist expertise.
- Local seasonal health relevance.
- Medical education and student community engagement.

The central challenge is converting academic authority into shareable, understandable, and trusted public-health communication while also engaging the medical student and faculty community.

## Benchmark Comparison Set

When benchmarking MedCMU, use the following comparator organizations and clearly label their type:

| Organization | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Siriraj Hospital (Mahidol) | Academic teaching hospital / medical faculty | Largest medical faculty in Thailand; national benchmark |
| Ramathibodi Hospital (Mahidol) | Academic teaching hospital / medical faculty | Strong research and specialist reputation |
| King Chulalongkorn Memorial Hospital (Chula Med) | Academic teaching hospital / medical faculty | Central Bangkok; high brand visibility |
| Samitivej Hospital | Private hospital group | High-engagement lifestyle health content; different mission |
| Bangkok Hospital | Private hospital group | National brand scale; well-resourced communications |

When comparing with private hospitals (Samitivej, Bangkok Hospital), note the mission difference: private hospitals optimize for patient acquisition and brand trust, while MedCMU prioritizes public-health value, research credibility, and community service. Do not penalize MedCMU for lower commercial-style engagement.

When comparing with academic peers (Siriraj, Ramathibodi, Chula Med), focus on content quality, research translation, and earned media authority.

## Priority Content Pillars

### Core Public Health (highest priority)
- PM2.5 and respiratory health (seasonal peak: November to March).
- Seasonal outbreaks and infectious disease (dengue, influenza, emerging pathogens).
- Cancer prevention and screening (Northern Thailand-relevant: liver cancer, lung cancer, cervical cancer).
- Elderly health and chronic disease management.
- Emergency care and hospital navigation.
- Maternal and child health.

### Hot Issues and Timely Content
- Current disease outbreaks or health alerts.
- National and regional public-health policy updates.
- Emerging health topics with high public concern.
- Crisis communication during disease events or hospital emergencies.

### Mental Health and Wellbeing (elevated priority)
- Staff wellbeing and burnout prevention (doctors, nurses, residents).
- Medical student mental health and peer support.
- Community mental health stigma reduction.
- Work-life balance in medical education.

### Medical Education and Student Life
- Medical student activities, milestones, and achievements.
- Student clubs, events, and community service.
- White Coat ceremonies, graduation, clinical rotations.
- Resident and intern program highlights.
- Faculty-student collaborative projects.

### Seasonal and Event-Based Content
- Academic year calendar events (welcome week, sports day, annual concerts, farewell).
- Northern Thailand festivals with health angles (Songkran water safety, Loy Krathong crowd safety).
- National health awareness months and days.
- Hospital anniversary and institutional milestones.

### General Health Literacy
- Everyday health questions the public asks (myth-busting, ask-the-doctor formats).
- Simple prevention tips for common conditions.
- Medication safety and drug information.
- Food, nutrition, and exercise basics relevant to Northern Thai lifestyle.

### Research Translation
- Translating faculty research into plain-language public posts.
- What this finding means for people in Northern Thailand.
- Specialist expertise made accessible.
- Medical innovation and new treatments at Maharaj Nakorn Hospital.

### Community Service and Rural Health Equity
- Mobile health units and outreach programs.
- Health access in rural and hill tribe communities.
- Telemedicine and referral network updates.

## Benchmark Interpretation

Do not judge MedCMU only by entertainment-style engagement. Also evaluate:

- Public-health usefulness and community health literacy improvement.
- Trust transfer from institution to community.
- Research translation quality and accessibility.
- Crisis readiness and response speed.
- Accessibility for Northern Thai audiences (including regional dialect and rural users).
- Earned amplification by news, community pages, and public-health networks.
- Student and faculty community engagement quality.
- Mission alignment: public service vs. commercial health content.

When private hospitals outperform MedCMU on raw engagement, analyze whether the content type is comparable. A hospital marketing campaign is not equivalent to a public-health education post.

## MedCMU Scoring Emphasis

For MedCMU, engagement efficiency is the primary scoring lens. How much meaningful interaction is generated per post and per follower matters more than total audience size. Large private hospital followings built on lifestyle content do not signal stronger institutional authority.

Secondary priorities:
- Content consistency: regular, reliable posting builds community trust over time.
- Strategic value: content that serves MedCMU's public mission scores higher than viral but low-value posts.

## 90-Day Improvement Themes

- Build PM2.5 seasonal content calendar before peak season (October onward).
- Convert specialist knowledge into short public explainers (TikTok/Reels format).
- Create recurring doctor-answers series with plain-language scripts.
- Pair research posts with what this means for people in Northern Thailand.
- Build earned amplification relationships with local media and community networks.
- Track saves, shares, and comment quality as trust indicators.
- Launch dedicated student life content series to build community and support recruitment.
- Create a mental health content series for staff, students, and the public.
- Build a hot-issue rapid response workflow: publish within 24 to 48 hours of trending health topics.
- Develop seasonal event content templates to reduce production time for recurring events.
