# Scoring Rubric

Use weighted scoring only after KPI formulas and data limitations are clear. Scores are decision support, not ground truth.

## Default Weights

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Engagement Efficiency | 25 |
| Engagement Quality | 20 |
| Follower Growth Strength | 15 |
| Growth Sustainability | 13 |
| Reach Power | 10 |
| Content Consistency | 10 |
| Strategic Value | 5 |
| Stability / Volatility Control | 2 |

Weights sum to 100.

> **MedCMU note**: Engagement efficiency is the lead dimension. How much meaningful interaction is generated per post and per follower is a stronger signal of institutional authority than raw audience size, especially when benchmarking against private hospitals with different content missions.

## Score Scale

- 5: excellent, strong evidence, sustained, efficient, and strategically valuable.
- 4: strong, mostly consistent with minor gaps.
- 3: moderate, acceptable but uneven or limited.
- 2: weak, clear underperformance or unreliable evidence.
- 1: critical, poor performance or insufficient basis for positive judgment.

## Confidence Grade

High:

- Same reporting period across platforms.
- Reach or impressions available.
- Follower start/end available.
- Paid/organic split known or not relevant.
- Enough posts for stable interpretation.

Medium:

- Some denominators missing.
- Paid/organic split unavailable.
- Earned media incomplete.
- Some platform periods differ.

Low:

- Only vanity metrics available.
- No reach/impression denominator.
- Competitor data not comparable.
- Major missing platform or period.
- Strong campaign spike with no baseline period.

## Scoring Rules

- Never give a high confidence score when denominator quality is low.
- Do not reward large follower base unless efficiency or growth is also strong.
- Do not reward reach spikes without sustainability or content quality.
- Penalize content risk even when engagement is high.
- Report tied or close scores as strategically equivalent rather than over-ranking.
- When benchmarking MedCMU against private hospitals, note mission differences before interpreting efficiency gaps.

## Sensitivity Analysis

Run sensitivity when:

- Top two platforms differ by less than 0.25 weighted points.
- User disputes strategic weights.
- A single dimension drives the final ranking.
- Data confidence is low.

Recommended scenarios:

- Engagement-efficiency-heavy weights (default for MedCMU).
- Public-health-value-heavy weights.
- Growth-heavy weights.
- Low-resource operational weights.
